LEVIATHAN
Social decisions

Leviathan is for making social decisions. This is subtly different from a group of individuals making a decision. It's like voting on things to get an idea of what people want, but it's not representative of the people that make up a group. Instead, Leviathan helps us make decisions differently.

Building on democratic tradition is a worthy cause, so one person, one vote is an ideal place to start. However we all have limits on what we know, and even on what we can imagine. A person can't meaningfully vote on things they know nothing about, and a group can't reasonably consider things they've never encountered. This is why we're often prejudiced - it's simple, easy, needs no experience, and we can all do it... but it's inaccurate.

Each of us may be an expert in one or two things, but all of us are beginners at just about everything. So to do better, what we really need is one person, one consideration. Leviathan won't necessarily represent you - it presents a decision that is a function of relevance, whether individuals think so or not. So, we make the statements; Leviathan presents the decisions. It represents us.

Like a telescope, Leviathan is a tool that helps give more accurate perspectives beyond our natural world view. if that sounds useful to you, then you should use it - but as with all tools, it's up to you to decide what to do with it.

Welcome to Leviathan

These notes are extra-detailed, for dev. Will be shortened / replaced with gifs for live. Some things that it says here can be done... maybe can't be done. And some things that are not said here may actually be possible.

Welcome to Leviathan. As you're not logged in you can try anything - like a demo mode, it works but nothing gets saved. When you're ready to really get started, just login.

Just tap the search bar above - you'll see filters by topics, users, groups, and more. Type or select your preferences, then hit enter or tap the search icon. You'll be shown the first of many relevant statements - swipe the statement left to respond negatively, or right to respond positively, or tap it to see more info before deciding.

Below the search is the response bar. Here you can tap the red minus button to quickly respond negatively to a statement, or the green plus button for positive response. When there are no statements on display, this is also where you can make your own - just tap in the response bar for more info.

When responding, you can optionally say a bit more - just write your own responding statement then tap red or green. Or instead of writing a response, tap the pink say button and select is - now the response bar gives dropdown options such as is priority, fake etc. You can also select and to match related statements, or select or to choose between pairs of statements. (Our user stories demonstrate and, or, is in more detail.)

If you want to browse all statements and responses, just select all.
